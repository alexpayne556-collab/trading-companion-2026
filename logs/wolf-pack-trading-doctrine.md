### 4. Insiders Know Best: Track Form 4 & Form 144
**The Edge No One Talks About**

**Form 4 (Transaction Report):**
- Officers/directors buying/selling
- Filed within 2 business days of trade
- GREEN: CEO/CFO buying = strong confidence
- RED: Multiple insiders selling = warning

**Form 144 (Intent to Sell Restricted Stock):**
- Pre-announcement (30-90 days before sale)
- Early warning system for supply
- CRITICAL: Large Form 144s = stock about to get hit

**Wolf Pack Monitoring Protocol:**
1. Check SEC EDGAR daily for target stocks
2. Set alerts for Form 4 and Form 144 filings
3. If insiders sell + price drops = tighten stops
4. If insiders buy + price rises = add to winners

**AR Example:**
- Check CIK: 0001433270 daily
- If CFO files Form 4 purchase ‚Üí bullish
- If 3+ officers file Form 144s ‚Üí caution

## II. Enemy Tactics: What Market Makers Do to You

### The Stop Hunt
**How it works:** MM sees stop cluster at $34, pushes price to $33.90, triggers stops, then reverses

**Defense:**
- Set stops at logical levels (below support), not round numbers
- Use wider stops on volatile stocks
- Accept that some stop hunts happen - better than no stop

### The Fake Breakout
**How it works:** Stock breaks resistance, you buy, immediately reverses (bull trap)

**Defense:**
- Wait for volume confirmation
- Let first 30 min of market settle (avoid open volatility)
- Require 3-5% follow-through before adding

### The Slow Bleed
**How it works:** Stock slowly drops without trigger stops, down 15-20% before you realize

**Defense:**
- Use time stops (if thesis hasn't played out in X days, exit)
- Use trailing stops (chandelier) to lock profits
- Reassess thesis weekly

### The Volatility Crush
**How it works:** You buy options, stock goes nowhere, theta decay kills you

**Defense:**
- Wolf Pack prefers shares over options (for most trades)
- If using options, buy 60+ DTE (days to expiration)
- Never hold options through earnings (IV crush)

## III. Execution Framework

### Pre-Market (6:00-9:30 AM ET)
1. Review overnight news (Bloomberg, CNBC, SEC filings)
2. Check futures (ES, NQ, NG)
3. Scan watchlist for setups
4. Review open positions, adjust stops if needed
5. Write down plan: "Today I will [X]"

### Market Hours (9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET)
**9:30-10:00 AM:** Let market settle, observe price action  
**10:00 AM-12:00 PM:** Execute planned entries (avoid FOMO chasing)  
**12:00-2:00 PM:** Lunch lull (low volume, avoid trading unless thesis-driven)  
**2:00-4:00 PM:** Manage positions, take profits if targets hit

### After Market (4:00-6:00 PM ET)
1. Journal trades (wins AND losses)
2. Scan SEC filings (Form 4, Form 144, 8-Ks)
3. Update watchlist (add/remove tickers)
4. Review thesis validator (did today's action confirm or deny?)
5. Plan tomorrow: "Tomorrow I will [X]"
