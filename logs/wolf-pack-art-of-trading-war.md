## I. The Legends - And How They Fell

### Jesse Livermore: The Boy Plunger
**Greatest Trade:** Shorted market before 1929 crash, made $100M (equiv. $1.5B today)  
**Trading Genius:**
- Pioneered tape reading
- Understood market psychology
- "Sit tight and be right"

**How He Died:**  
**Suicide, 1940. Broke. Again.**

**What Killed Him:**
1. **Ego after success** - thought he was invincible after 1929
2. **No position sizing** - bet too big after wins
3. **Averaged down losers** - "it must come back"
4. **Personal expenses** - lived like a king, traded to maintain lifestyle
5. **No stop losses in later years** - "I know better"

**Wolf Pack Lesson:**  
*"Your greatest enemy is your last big win."*  
- After huge win, REDUCE size (don't increase)
- Detach lifestyle from trading (don't trade to pay bills)
- Stop loss is ALWAYS sacred, even after 100 wins

### Bill Hwang & Archegos Capital: $20 Billion Lost in 2 Days

**The Setup (2020-2021):**
- Hwang ran family office (less regulation)
- Used **total return swaps** (synthetic exposure, 5-10x leverage)
- Concentrated in ViacomCBS, Discovery, Baidu, GSX
- Grew from $1.5B to $20B (paper wealth)

**The Fall (March 2021):**
- ViacomCBS announced stock offering ‚Üí price dropped
- Margin calls triggered across banks
- Archegos couldn't meet calls
- Banks liquidated positions
- Domino effect: VIAC -50%, DIS -45%, BIDU -30% in DAYS
- Total loss: $20B+ (Hwang's fortune), $10B+ (bank losses)

**What Killed Him:**
1. **Leverage on leverage** - 5-10x through swaps
2. **Concentration** - same stocks across multiple swaps
3. **No stops** - held through margin calls
4. **Hubris** - ignored warnings from banks
5. **Liquidity illusion** - "I can always sell" (narrator: he couldn't)

**Wolf Pack Lesson:**  
*"Leverage is a double-edged chainsaw."*
- Max 2% risk per trade (NO leverage on risk)
- Diversify across sectors, not just tickers
- If you can't sell it in 1 day, position is TOO BIG
- Margin calls = game over (avoid margin entirely for small accounts)

### Long-Term Capital Management (LTCM): Nobel Prize Winners Blow Up

**The Dream Team (1994-1998):**
- Founders: John Meriwether (ex-Salomon), Myron Scholes & Robert Merton (Nobel Prize)
- Strategy: Arbitrage "risk-free" spreads (convergence trades)
- Returns: 40%+ annually (1994-1997)
- Leverage: 25-to-1 (later 100-to-1)

**The Collapse (August-September 1998):**
- Russia defaulted on debt (Aug 17, 1998)
- "Impossible" spreads widened instead of converging
- LTCM had $125B in assets, $1.25 **TRILLION** in derivatives exposure
- Lost $4.6B in 4 months
- Fed orchestrated $3.6B bailout to prevent systemic collapse

**What Killed Them:**
1. **"This can't happen" mindset** - models said Russia default = 1 in 10,000 years
2. **Leverage on "sure things"** - 100-to-1 leverage because "risk-free"
3. **Correlation assumption** - thought all positions were uncorrelated (they weren't)
4. **Ignored tail risk** - "6-sigma events don't happen" (narrator: they do)
5. **Arrogance** - Nobel Prize winners ignored common sense

**Wolf Pack Lesson:**  
*"Markets can remain irrational longer than you can remain solvent."*
- "Can't happen" WILL happen eventually
- Black swans are not 1-in-10,000 - they're 1-in-20
- Math models are tools, not gospel
- Stop loss protects against the unknowable

### Paul Tudor Jones: The Living Legend

**Why He's Still Here:**
1. **Defense first** - "Losers average losers"
2. **5:1 rule** - only takes trades with 5:1 reward-risk minimum
3. **Cuts fast** - famous for exiting at -5% (doesn't wait for stop)
4. **Size down after losses** - reduces position size after bad trade
5. **Obsessed with risk** - "At the end of the day, protection of capital is key"

**Greatest Trade:**
- Predicted & profited from 1987 Black Monday crash
- Made ~$100M personally, fund returned 200% that year
- How? Positioned short into overvalued market, added as it fell

**Wolf Pack Lesson:**  
*"You make money by NOT losing it."*
- Target 5:1 (or at least 3:1) setups
- Cut at -5% if thesis clearly wrong (don't wait for full stop)
- After loss, trade SMALLER (not bigger)

## II. Small Account Success Stories

### How They Turned $10K into $1M+ Without Blowing Up

**Common Traits:**
1. **Risk management** - never more than 2-3% per trade
2. **Patience** - waited for A+ setups, didn't trade daily
3. **Scaling** - added to winners, cut losers fast
4. **Specialization** - mastered 1-2 strategies (not jack-of-all-trades)
5. **Journaling** - obsessive tracking of every trade

**Example: The Momentum Trader**
- Started with $10K
- Strategy: Only traded stocks breaking 52-week highs with volume
- Position size: 2% risk per trade
- Win rate: 45% (but avg win = 15%, avg loss = 5%)
- Timeframe: 18 months to $100K, 3 years to $500K
- Key: NEVER revenge traded, sat out after 2 losses in a row

**Example: The Options Swing Trader**
- Started with $15K
- Strategy: Bought 60+ DTE calls on stocks with bullish catalysts
- Risk: Max $300 per trade (2% of $15K)
- Win rate: 40% (but winners were 3-5x, losers capped at -100%)
- Timeframe: 2 years to $150K
- Key: NEVER held through earnings, always sold winners at 100%+ gain

**Wolf Pack Lesson:**  
*"Consistency > home runs. Survival > glory."*
- Small account = focus on NOT blowing up
- 2% risk means you can survive 20 consecutive losses (you won't have 20)
- Compounding is your edge: 5% monthly = 80% yearly = double in 15 months

## III. The Psychological Killers

### Revenge Trading
**What it is:** Losing trade ‚Üí emotional ‚Üí immediately enter new trade to "get even"

**Why it kills:**
- No plan, pure emotion
- Often bigger size ("I'll make it back faster")
- Usually in same direction (doubling down)

**Antidote:**
- After loss, mandatory 30-min break
- After 2 losses, stop trading for day
- Journal the loss before entering new trade

---

### Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)
**What it is:** Stock rips without you ‚Üí panic buy at top ‚Üí immediate loss

**Why it kills:**
- Chasing = buying at resistance, not support
- No thesis, just "it's going up"
- Usually tops out right after you buy

**Antidote:**
- "If I missed it, the next train is coming"
- Wait for pullback to support before entering
- Keep watchlist of 10-20 stocks, something is ALWAYS setting up

---

### Anchoring (The "It Must Come Back" Trap)
**What it is:** Bought at $50, now $30, "it'll come back to $50"

**Why it kills:**
- Market doesn't care what you paid
- Holding losers prevents capital from working elsewhere
- "Come back" often means "go to $10"

**Antidote:**
- Stop loss is THE antidote
- If stopped out, ask: "Would I buy this RIGHT NOW at this price?"
- If no, why are you holding it?

---

### Overtrading (The "I Must Do Something" Disease)
**What it is:** Trading because markets are open, not because setup exists

**Why it kills:**
- Death by 1000 cuts (commissions, slippage, mistakes)
- Exhaustion leads to poor decisions
- Best trades are obvious, not forced

**Antidote:**
- "Cash is a position"
- Set rule: Max 3 entries per day (forces selectivity)
- Journal "no-trade days" as wins

## IV. Advanced Techniques: The Chandelier Exit

**What it is:** Trailing stop that hangs from highest point like a chandelier

**How it works:**
1. Enter position at $36.50
2. Stock rises to $40 (new high)
3. Set trailing stop at $40 - (8% of $40) = $36.80
4. Stock rises to $45 (new high)
5. Move stop to $45 - (8% of $45) = $41.40
6. Stock falls, triggers stop at $41.40 ‚Üí exit with profit

**Why 8%?**
- Enough room for normal volatility
- Not so wide that you give back all gains
- Adjustable: use 5-6% for volatile stocks, 10% for stable grinders

**Wolf Pack Application (AR Trade):**
- Enter: $36.50
- T1 ($40): Sell 25%, move stop to breakeven
- T2 ($44): Sell 35%, set chandelier at $44 - 8% = $40.48
- If rises to $48: Chandelier at $48 - 8% = $44.16
- Trail until stopped

**Key:** Chandelier locks in profits while letting winners run
