# PART II: THE PHILOSOPHY
## Core Principles That Guide Everything

---

## PRINCIPLE 1: LLHR - LIVE LONG, HUNT RELENTLESSLY

The wolf doesn't hunt once and retire. The wolf hunts every day, for life. But a dead wolf hunts nothing.

**Live Long** = Protect capital. Don't blow up. Survive the bad days so you're there for the good ones. Position sizing, stop losses, not betting the whole war chest on one play.

**Hunt Relentlessly** = Never stop looking for opportunities. Always be learning. Always be improving. The market offers chances every single day - be ready to take them.

LLHR is the balance between aggression and survival. Too cautious = slow death from a thousand missed opportunities. Too aggressive = one bad trade ends everything.

**The goal: Aggressive enough to grow. Disciplined enough to last.**

---

## PRINCIPLE 2: THE DJANGO METHOD

From the movie Django Unchained. The scene where Django, a freed slave, must watch something horrific happen and not react. Dr. King Schultz tells him he must play a role, suppress his emotions, or the mission fails.

**In trading, this means:**

- When a stock drops 10% after you buy, you don't panic sell
- When you miss a 50% runner, you don't FOMO into the next one
- When you're up 30%, you don't get cocky and over-leverage
- When someone online says your stock is trash, you don't abandon your thesis

**The market is designed to trigger emotions.** Every red candle wants you to feel fear. Every green candle wants you to feel FOMO. Every missed trade wants you to feel regret.

Django watches. Django waits. Django acts with purpose, not emotion.

**You are Django.**

---

## PRINCIPLE 3: CONVICTION VS SPECULATION

Not all trades are equal. Know which kind you're making.

**CONVICTION TRADES (Thesis Plays)**
- Based on research, insider activity, fundamental catalysts
- Held through volatility because the thesis is intact
- Sized bigger because confidence is higher
- Stop loss is based on thesis invalidation, not price noise
- Example: AISP - 9 insider buys, Paul Allen in at $2.74, government contracts

**SPECULATION TRADES (Momentum Plays)**
- Based on momentum, sector heat, quick setups
- Sized smaller because confidence is lower
- Exited quickly - take profits, don't marry the trade
- Stop loss is tight because there's no thesis to protect
- Example: SIDU after-hours - caught the momentum, house money play

**The danger:** Treating speculation like conviction (holding garbage hoping it comes back) or treating conviction like speculation (selling AISP on a 2% dip when Paul Allen is still holding).

**Know which trade you're in.**

---

## PRINCIPLE 4: SCARED MONEY VS STUPID MONEY

There are two ways to lose in markets:

**Scared Money (Never Deploying)**
- Sitting in 100% cash waiting for the "perfect" setup
- Watching winners run without you
- Analysis paralysis
- Fear of any loss preventing any gain
- Death by a thousand missed opportunities

**Stupid Money (Over-Deploying)**
- Going all-in on one trade
- No stop losses
- Averaging down into losers
- Betting money you can't afford to lose
- Death by one catastrophic loss

**The Wolf Pack Way:**
- Stay 60-70% deployed in validated setups
- Keep 30-40% cash for opportunities
- Never put more than 20% in a single position
- Always have stops (even mental ones)
- Use TODAY'S PROFITS for speculation (house money)

---

## PRINCIPLE 5: THE THESIS IS EVERYTHING

Before entering any trade, answer: **"Why am I buying this?"**

If the answer is "because it's going up" - that's momentum, size small, exit fast.

If the answer is "because insiders are buying, the sector is hot, there's a catalyst coming, and the technicals show accumulation" - that's a thesis, size bigger, hold through noise.

**The thesis determines:**
- Position size
- Hold time
- Stop loss placement
- When to add vs when to exit

**Thesis intact?** Hold. Add on dips. Trust the process.

**Thesis broken?** Exit. Don't hope. Don't pray. Move on.

Example: AISP thesis is "Paul Allen and 8 other insiders bought in December at $2.74. Government defense contracts. AI integration. Catalyst: Q4 earnings."

That thesis breaks if: Insiders start SELLING, major contract cancelled, Allen exits position, price breaks below $2.30 (below insider cost basis).

Until one of those happens, noise is just noise.

---

## PRINCIPLE 6: WINNERS VS LOSERS BEHAVIOR

**What Losers Do:**
- Cut winners fast, let losers run (hoping for recovery)
- Add to losing positions (averaging down into disaster)
- No stop losses (hope is not a strategy)
- Trade on emotion (FOMO, fear, revenge)
- Blame the market, blame manipulation, blame anything but themselves
- Look for someone to tell them what to buy

**What Winners Do:**
- Let winners run, cut losers fast
- Add to winners (averaging up into strength)
- Always have an exit plan before entry
- Trade on thesis (can articulate why they're in)
- Take responsibility for every outcome
- Do their own research, make their own decisions

**Be honest about which list describes you. Then change.**

## STOP LOSS PHILOSOPHY

Stops protect the THESIS, not the FEAR.

**For Conviction Plays:**
- Stop below the level where thesis breaks
- For AISP: $2.30 (below Paul Allen's $2.74 entry)
- Wider stop because you trust the thesis
- Willing to take more heat

**For Speculation Plays:**
- Tight stops - 5-8% max
- You have no thesis to protect
- If it doesn't work quickly, move on
- Protect profits aggressively

**Stop-Limit vs Stop-Market:**
- Stop-Limit: Set stop trigger at $X, limit sell at $Y (won't sell below Y)
- Gap risk: If it gaps below your limit, order won't fill
- Stop-Market: Sells at any price once trigger hits
- Guaranteed exit but might get bad fill

**Our Default:** Stop-Limit with 10-15 cent buffer (Stop at $2.30, Limit at $2.20)

## EXIT STRATEGIES

**Taking Profits:**
- First target: 10-15% gain - consider trimming 25-50%
- Second target: 25%+ gain - trim to house money position

**Cutting Losses:**
- Stop hit: EXIT. No questions. No hoping.
- Thesis broken: EXIT even if stop not hit
- Better opportunity: Rotate capital to higher conviction play

**The Trailing Stop Method:**
- Once up 15%+, move stop to breakeven
- Once up 25%+, trail stop at 15% below high
- Let the market take you out at a profit

- Lower entry, similar upside
